# Interview Rounds — Question Bank

This file guides the interview process in deepwork-01-discovery. The goal is not to ask all questions — it's to ask the **right** questions for this specific project until each topic area is genuinely clear.

Use `AskUserQuestion` with as many rounds as needed. 2–4 questions per round works well. Stop when you have enough to research intelligently.

---

## Round A — What & Why

1. **Problem:** What's the exact problem you're solving? Describe it without mentioning a solution — what's broken, missing, or painful right now?
2. **For whom:** Who will use or benefit from this? Be specific — "developers" is too vague, "solo indie developers who ship without a team" is useful.
3. **Pain intensity:** How bad is the problem without this? What do people do instead today? What's wrong with that workaround?
4. **Success criterion:** How would you know — in a binary way — that this worked?

---

## Round B — Scope & Constraints

5. **Time budget:** How much time realistically can go into this? Hours? Weeks? Months? Is there a deadline?
6. **Money budget:** Is there a cost limit for infrastructure, APIs, licenses, or tooling?
7. **Tech preferences:** Any hard requirements? Must use X. Can't use Y.
8. **Hard Don'ts:** What would be a dealbreaker if the final solution included it?
9. **Integration requirements:** What does this need to connect to or coexist with?

---

## Round C — Context

10. **Your role:** What are you to this project? Owner, developer, PM, all three?
11. **Existing assets:** What do you already have that's relevant? Existing code, data, research, designs?
12. **Past attempts:** Has this been tried before — by you or others? What happened?
13. **Why now:** What changed that makes this the right time?

---

## Round D — Known Unknowns

14. **Biggest uncertainty:** What's the thing you're most unsure about?
15. **Assumptions:** What are you assuming to be true that you haven't actually verified? List at least 3.
16. **Risk tolerance:** If a key assumption turns out to be wrong, what do you do? Pivot, cut scope, or abandon?

---

## Re-Interview Triggers (after research)

Run a targeted re-interview round when research uncovers:
- **Alternative approaches** that weren't mentioned
- **Common failure modes** in this domain
- **Assumptions that look shaky**
- **Regulatory/legal landscape** changes
- **Competitors or references** not previously considered

Limit to max 2 re-interview rounds. If questions keep multiplying, note them as Open Issues and move forward.

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

**Too abstract:** "What do you want this to do?" → Push for specifics.  
**Too leading:** "So you want a web app, right?" → Let them describe first.  
**Too many at once:** More than 5 questions per round → user overwhelm.  
**Circular:** Re-asking already-answered questions after research → check decisions.md first.  
**Endless:** More than 4 total interview rounds → note Open Issues and move on.
